What Is Solidarity Tech?

Solidarity Tech is a centralized organizing platform designed to help volunteers like you coordinate campaigns, manage events, and track organizing efforts. Whether you're working at the dashboard, on mobile, or sharing sign-up pages with supporters, Solidarity Tech is your command center for volunteer engagement.

Three Ways to Use Solidarity Tech

The Dashboard (Website)

Your main command center is at dashboard.solidarity.tech. This is where you'll do most of your organizing work, from creating events to managing volunteers.

Figure 1: The Solidarity Tech Dashboard
The Mobile App

Organize on the go! Download the mobile app at solidarity.tech/app available on both iOS and Android.

Figure 2: The Mobile App
Sign-Up Pages

These are the web pages supporters see when they're signing up to volunteer. You create these through Solidarity Tech and share them on social media, flyers, or emails to recruit volunteers.

Figure 3: Sign-Up Page for Supporters

What You'll Do with Solidarity Tech

  • Create and manage events
  • Recruit and track volunteers
  • Coordinate volunteer recruitment efforts (email, phone banking, texting*)

*Email, phone banking, and texting features will be available at a later date.

What Solidarity Tech Does NOT Do

  • No voter data - this is volunteer organizing only
  • It does not replace VoteBuilder - use Solidarity Tech for volunteers, VoteBuilder for voters
Think of Solidarity Tech as your volunteer management system. It helps you organize volunteers, but doesn't handle voter information or voter outreach.

How to Log In

Getting access to Solidarity Tech is a three-step process: receive an invitation, create your account, and log in. Let's walk through each step.

Step 1: Receive Your Invitation

To get started with Solidarity Tech, your DEC chair or the FDP Data Team will add you to the system. You'll receive an invitation via text or email with a link to set up your account.

Figure 4: Example Invitation

Step 2: Create Your Account

Click the invitation link, and you'll be taken to a page where you can set up your account. Enter your email, create a password, and click Submit.

Figure 5: Account Creation
You must complete account creation by clicking the invitation link and submitting your details. Simply being in the contacts database is not enough to log in. You need your own user account.

Step 3: Log In

Now you're ready to log in!

  • On Computer: Go to dashboard.solidarity.tech
  • On Phone: Download the app from solidarity.tech/app (iOS App Store or Google Play)
Figure 6: Dashboard Login
Bookmark dashboard.solidarity.tech in your browser so you can quickly access it in the future.

Forgot Your Password?

If you forget your password, follow these steps:

  • Go to the login page
  • Click "Forgot Password"
  • Enter your email address
  • Check your email for a password reset link
Figure 7: Password Reset
Password reset only works if you've already created your account by clicking the invitation link. If you never completed account setup, ask your DEC chair or the FDP Data Team to resend your invitation.

Part C: Adding Sessions

Events can have one or more sessions. Each session has its own start time, end time, location, and title. This is useful for recurring events or events with multiple time slots.

Adding a Single Session

Follow these steps to add a session to your event:

  1. Open your event
  2. Click on the "Schedule" tab
  3. Click "Add Session"
  4. Set the session start and end times
  5. Set the session location (can be different from event location)
  6. Set the session title (optional)
  7. Click "Create"
  8. Your session is added to the event
Figure 18: Schedule Tab with Add Session Button
Figure 19: Adding a Session
If you edit a session after people have already RSVP'd, the system will ask if you want to send updated calendar invitations to everyone who signed up.
Making an Event Recurring

For events that happen on a regular schedule (weekly meetings, daily shifts), you can set them to repeat automatically:

  1. Create your first event session as normal
  2. Click "Add Session" again
  3. Instead of adding a single session, look for "Make Recurring"
  4. Choose the recurrence pattern: Daily, Weekly, or Monthly
  5. Set when the recurring sessions should end
  6. Click "Create"
  7. Solidarity Tech will automatically create all the sessions

Part E: Event Settings

Before creating the event page and sharing your event, review the settings to make sure everything is configured correctly. At the bottom right of the page select Edit to view the event settings.

  • Languages: Set what languages information about this event will be available in
  • Event/Campaign Tags: Tag your event for organizing and filtering (e.g., "Private" to hide from main calendar)
  • Enable RSVP Confirmation: Adds a "Confirm" button that appears before the event for a more accurate headcount
  • Enable Host Tools: Generate a public link for event hosts to view RSVPs, mark attendance, and contact attendees without logging in

Events: Managing RSVPs & Attendance

Once your event is live and volunteers are signing up, you need to manage who's coming and take attendance. Here's how to do it.

Checking Who Signed Up

To see all the volunteers who RSVP'd to your event:

  1. Click "Events" in the sidebar
  2. Click on your event
  3. Click the "RSVPs" tab
  4. You'll see everyone who signed up. You can filter by session if you have multiple time slots with the dropdown in the upper right.
Figure 30: Viewing RSVPs

Checking People In

When people arrive at your event, you need to mark them as present. There are two ways to do this:

Method 1: Using Host Tools

If you've enabled Host Tools for your event, volunteers can check people in using a simple interface:

  1. At the event, give volunteers the Host Tools link
  2. They can search for volunteer names
  3. Click the name to mark them checked in
  4. To add walk-ins (people who weren't on the RSVP list), click the + icon and add their info
Figure 31: Host Tools Check-In Screen
Method 2: Using Attendance Screen

From the dashboard, you can also take attendance directly:

  1. Open your event
  2. Click "Take Attendance"
  3. Search for volunteers by name or phone number
  4. Click their name to mark them checked in
Figure 32: Dashboard Attendance Screen

Someone Shows Up Who Isn't in the System?

No problem! During check-in, if someone isn't in the system:

  1. Search their name or phone number
  2. If they're not found, click "Add New Contact"
  3. Enter their information
  4. Mark them checked in
This is a great way to capture new volunteer contacts at events. Every person who shows up gets added to your volunteer database automatically.

Self Check-In with QR Code

For a hands-off approach, you can generate a QR code that volunteers can scan to check themselves in:

  1. Open your event session
  2. Click the Edit button
  3. Find the "Self Check-In" section
  4. Copy the link or download the QR code
  5. Print the QR code and post it at your event
  6. Volunteers can scan it from their phone to check in

How to Find Someone

To look up a volunteer:

  1. Click "People" in the sidebar
  2. Use the filters on the left to narrow down your list (optional)
  3. Click on a person's name to open their profile
Figure 35: Finding Contacts in People Section

What You See on a Profile

When you click on someone, you'll see their volunteer profile with lots of useful information:

Figure 36: Volunteer Profile

A profile includes:

  • Main Details: Name and Assessment
  • Activity Stream: Recent actions and communications
  • Contact Details: Phone, email, address
  • Chapters: Which groups or areas they're involved with
  • Language: Preferred language for communications

In the People section, you have several ways to view and organize contacts:

  • Columns: Choose which information to display
  • Map View: See volunteers plotted on a map
  • Chart View: Visualize data about your volunteers

People: Searching & Filtering

With a large volunteer list, you need powerful tools to find exactly who you're looking for. Solidarity Tech's filtering system makes it easy. This same filter is in multiple places in Solidarity Tech but the functionality is the same.

How to Use the Filter

Here's the step-by-step process for filtering your contacts:

  1. Click "People" in the sidebar
  2. Click the "Filter" button (usually a funnel icon)
  3. Click "Add Filter" to start building rules
  4. Select a property to filter by (Name, Location, Event RSVP'd to, etc.)
  5. Choose an operator: "is", "is not", "includes", "excludes", etc.
  6. Enter the value you're looking for
  7. To add more rules, click "Add Filter" again
  8. Choose whether rules should use AND or OR logic
  9. Click "Apply" to see results
Figure 37: Advanced Filter Interface
Use "includes" for properties with multiple options (like Volunteer Type). Use "is" for specific single-value fields.

Useful Filters

Here are some common filters to help you find volunteers:

What You Want How to Filter
New sign-ups from last week Created At is within last 7 days
People who RSVP'd to a specific event RSVP'd Events includes [Event Name]
People who actually attended an event Attended Events includes [Event Name]
Volunteers in a certain area Location is [Zip Code/City] or Legislative Districts is [Congressional, Senate, House]
Volunteers for specific events or projects Custom User Property: FDP - Volunteer Type includes includes [Type]

Saving a Filter as a List

Once you've created a filter you like, you can save it to use again and share with your team:

  1. Create your filter (as described above)
  2. Click "Save as List"
  3. Give your list a name (e.g., "New Volunteers This Week")
  4. Your team can now use this saved filter

Troubleshooting & Common Questions

Got a problem? Here are answers to the most common questions volunteers ask about Solidarity Tech.

Login Problems

Solution: Contact your DEC chair. They need to add you to the Solidarity Tech system. Once they do, you'll receive an invitation via text or email. Check your spam folder if you don't see it right away.

Quick Reference: Login Troubleshooting

Problem Solution
No invitation received Contact your DEC chair to be added to the system
Can't log in after invitation Make sure you completed account setup; try password reset
Invitation link expired Ask your DEC chair to resend the invitation
Getting error messages when logging in Contact FDP Data Team or Solidarity Tech Support

Missing Events or Contacts

Problem: I can't find an event or contact I'm looking for.

Solution: Check your scope (top left corner of dashboard). You might be viewing the wrong organization's data. Switch to the correct organization in your scope selector.

Event Issues

Problem: Event types cannot be changed after creation.

Solution: For hybrid events, create the event as In-Person and include the virtual meeting link in your event description, emails, and SMS messages to attendees. Alternatively, delete the event and create a new one with the correct type (note: this will cancel any RSVPs).

Permissions Issues

Problem: A button is grayed out and I can't do something I need to do.

Solution: This means your role doesn't include that permission. Talk to your DEC chair about getting a role upgrade. Here's the quick reference:

  • Can't see People? Your role might be User or Organizer - need Admin
  • Can't create events? Your role might be User - need Organizer or Admin
  • Can't send emails (only draft)? Your role might be Organizer - need Admin
